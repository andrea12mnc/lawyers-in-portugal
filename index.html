Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about immigration to Portugal

Portugal offers several visa options including the Golden Visa (investment), D7 Visa (passive income/retirement), D2 Visa (entrepreneurs), and Digital Nomad Visa. Each has different requirements based on your financial situation and goals.

Processing times vary by visa type and individual circumstances. Generally, you can expect 3-6 months from application to approval. Golden Visa applications may take longer due to investment verification procedures.

Applicants must demonstrate a minimum stable income equivalent to the Portuguese minimum wage (currently around €760 per month). However, authorities often prefer to see higher income to ensure financial stability.

Yes, most Portuguese visas allow for family reunification. This typically includes your spouse, dependent children under 18 (or older if studying), and in some cases, dependent parents. Family members can apply simultaneously or later through family reunification.

To apply for Portuguese citizenship, you generally need to have legally resided in Portugal for 5 years, demonstrate basic Portuguese language proficiency (A2 level), have no criminal record, and show sufficient integration into Portuguese society.
